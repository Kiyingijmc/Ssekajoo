In Uganda, education remains a privilege, especially girls. While primary education is now universal, only few can
afford to go to a secondary school. Majority of the girls can’t afford scholastic materials from books, uniforms,
pencil and pens and cater for their feeding at school. For girls every day they face barriers caused by high
levels of poverty, early marriages, teenage pregnancies or child labor to increase on the families income. Educating
girls becomes crucial to create an equal society and sustainable development. However, educating girls goes
beyond getting them into school. It is about creating an environment in which they can feel save, grow and run through all levels of education to be equipped with the necessary skills to compete in life.

  • Distributing Imperial Reusable Sanitary Pads And Awareness Creating On Menstrual Hygiene

    Caring Hands Of Hope continued to created awareness on menstrual hygiene management in 5 rural schools and distributed Imperial reusable sanitary towels to 250 girls in reproductive age. The girls were further empowered to attend to menstruation without shame, ensuring they can experience their periods with dignity and fulfil their sexual and reproductive health and rights. The intervention is hoped to improve school attendance for girls and consequently reduce school dropout rates.
